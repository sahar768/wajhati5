Sacred Mosque

The Haram in Mecca is the heart of Mecca itself, and it's considered exceptionally sacred for Muslims. Muslims gather in the Haram to perform acts of worship like prayer and circumambulation around the Kaaba. Being there is seen as an opportunity to connect with God and achieve spiritual fulfillment. It also attracts believers from all over the world for performing Umrah and Hajj, making it a sacred hub for Islamic unity and solidarity.

Light mountain

Jabal al-Nour, also known as Mount Hira, is a rocky mountain located near Mecca in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. It holds great religious significance in Islam, as it is home to the Cave of Hira, where Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) engaged in worship and received the first revelations from Allah before the commencement of his prophethood. This makes it an important site for visitation and contemplation for Muslims.

Clock Tower Museum

The Clock Tower Museum is located in the Clock Tower in the center of the city of Mecca, Saudi Arabia. The museum houses a collection of historical and artistic pieces that reflect the history and culture of Mecca and its development over the ages. The Clock Tower itself is a prominent landmark in Mecca, and the museum is an interesting destination for visitors interested in exploring the city's history and heritage.
